\section{Weight Optimization via Constrained Least Squares}

The methodology theoretically constructs the counterfactual as a weighted average of control unit returns implementing a Constrained Least Squares (CLS) logic that optimizes direct return-matching across strictly defined prior validation windows.

To systematically prevent data contamination and arbitrary overfitting, testing utilizes discrete trailing periods anchored prior to the $T_0$ signal event:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Training Window}: $[min(T_0 - 84\text{h}), T_0 - 36\text{h}]$ (optimizing CLS control array alignments)
    \item \textbf{Hold-Out Window}: $[T_0 - 36\text{h}, T_0 - 12\text{h}]$ (out-of-sample parametric $R^2$ validation tracking evaluation)
\end{itemize}

A formal 12-hour gap directly preceding explicit manifestation at $T_0$ preemptively prevents potential preliminary internal coordination leaks from corrupting empirical baseline assessments.

The weight vector $\boldsymbol{\omega}$ strictly ensures that synthesized baseline evaluations hypothetically represent a physically viable, fully-subscribed longitudinal portfolio through explicit convexity restraints.

\begin{equation}
    \min_{\boldsymbol{\omega}} \| \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{X}\boldsymbol{\omega} \|_2^2
    \quad \text{subject to} \quad
    \sum_{k} \omega_k = 1, \quad \boldsymbol{\omega} \geq 0
\end{equation}

Logarithmic returns drive the optimization natively due to longitudinal absolute price non-stationarity—even fractional discrepancies across drift vectors dramatically decouple macro-tracked paths during multiday evaluations.

\section{Limitations of Cumulative Abnormal Returns (CAR)}
\label{sec:app-car-limitations}

Early architectural iterations of this study deployed Cumulative Abnormal Returns (CAR) to enforce a rigid macroeconomic isolation gap on the outcome variable:
\begin{equation}
CAR_{i,\Delta} = r_{target,i}^{[0,\Delta]} - r_{control,i}^{[0,\Delta]}
\label{eq:car-original}
\end{equation}
where $r_{target}^{[0,\Delta]}$ structurally bounded the real log-return and $r_{control}^{[0,\Delta]}$ applied a synthetic approximation mapping established market drift.

However, applying this synthetic control method to the sample proved ineffective. The average $R^2$ between the synthetic controls and actual pre-pump prices was close to zero, explicitly mapped in Figure~\ref{fig:app-r2-distribution}. Within the micro-cap and small-cap domains natively prioritized by manipulation syndicates, the predictive fits collapsed.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{figures/ch4/r2_distribution_by_mcap.png}
\caption{Distribution of $R^2$ scores across market capitalization strata. The fits remain universally depressed ($R^2 \approx 0$), indicating extreme tracking error in applying synthetic abstractions over micro-scale assets.}
\label{fig:app-r2-distribution}
\end{figure}

Because the synthetic controls did not actually track the target coins, using CAR made the problem worse. Instead of reducing market noise, subtracting an uncorrelated control variable simply increases the total sample variance:
\begin{equation}
\text{Var}(CAR) = \text{Var}(r_{target}) + \text{Var}(r_{control}) - 2\text{Cov}(r_{target}, r_{control})
\end{equation}
When the covariance matrix $\text{Cov}(r_{target}, r_{control})$ anchors to absolute zero, CAR inherently registers higher overall variance than isolated target returns, artificially masking true statistical influence beneath expanded noise.

Filtering the sample to only include valid synthetic controls (e.g., $R^2 > 0.25$) would discard over 75\% of the observed tracking events. Worse, this rejection logic would disproportionately remove low-liquidity coins, completely eliminating the entire micro-cap tier ($<$\$10M market capitalization). Such a maneuver forcibly limits the analytical purview exclusively toward large-cap, efficiently traded assets—the exact ecosystem fraction categorically hostile to Telegram coordinated manipulation. Consequently, the primary analysis structurally abandons CAR in favor of standard logarithmic returns natively mapped through Double Machine Learning models properly interacting the pre-event macroeconomic conditions without artificial subtraction.

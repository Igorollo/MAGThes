\begin{center}
  \textbf{Abstract}
\end{center}
\noindent Telegram-coordinated cryptocurrency pump-and-dump events are analyzed by linking signal features, high-frequency exchange data, and asset metadata. The empirical design estimates how \textit{Hype Intensity}, \textit{Urgency Index}, and \textit{Instruction Density} relate to the \textit{10-Minute Logarithmic Return} using Double Machine Learning and heterogeneity checks. Estimated effects differ by market capitalization. \textit{Urgency Index} is most clearly positive among small-cap assets, while several effects weaken or turn negative in larger markets. An XGBoost model evaluates whether the binary \textit{Success}/\textit{Non-Success} outcome can be classified from information available before the reveal. The findings characterize pump signals as short-term coordination tools whose impact depends on market conditions.
